Gilding

Gilding is the application of thin sheets of gold (or silver) to a surface. It was more frequently applied in medieval illumination, in the form of gold leaf, but was also applied to other objects.

Gilding formed the first stage in the painting process of illumination. Since it was a messy activity, the gilded area often required trimming with a knife. The gilding of a manuscript illustration was carried out by the artist or by a specialist.
